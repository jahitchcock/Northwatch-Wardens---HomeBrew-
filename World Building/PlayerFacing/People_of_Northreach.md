# People of Northreach

\page

## Overview

Northreach is shaped by its people—hardy folk who've chosen to make lives on the frontier. Here you'll find those who've earned their reputations through deeds rather than birth, and whose stories are woven into the region's daily rhythms.

---

## Guild Leaders

### Marshal Brenna Thorne

**Role:** Leader of the Northwatch Wardens

**Background:** Brenna came to Northreach fifteen years ago after a long career as a military officer in the southern territories. She saw an opportunity to build something meaningful—a guild that could protect people who had nowhere else to turn.

**Reputation:** Fair but firm. Brenna doesn't tolerate cowardice, but she respects honest fear. She's known for personally vetting every contract that comes through the guild and for standing by her wardens when things go wrong.

**Common Sayings:**
- "The work matters more than the glory."
- "I don't care who you were. I care who you are."

**What People Say About Her:**
- "Marshal Thorne doesn't ask anyone to do anything she wouldn't do herself."
- "She's got a memory like a steel trap—cross her once, and she'll remember."
- "Fair pay for fair work. That's her way."

---

### Steward Mara Fenwick

**Role:** Quartermaster and logistics coordinator for the Wardens

**Background:** Mara was a merchant's daughter who learned the value of a copper piece early. When her family's trading company failed, she found work with the Wardens and discovered she had a talent for keeping people equipped and paid on time.

**Reputation:** Efficient and no-nonsense. Mara knows exactly what's in the guild's stores at any given moment, and she's not afraid to tell a warden when they're asking for more than they need.

**Common Sayings:**
- "You want it? Prove you need it."
- "Every copper spent is a copper earned somewhere."

**What People Say About Her:**
- "Mara knows the price of everything and the value of most things."
- "Don't try to negotiate with her. You'll lose."
- "She's the reason the guild hasn't gone bankrupt."

---

### Lorewarden Elric Vael

**Role:** Record keeper, scholar, and arcane advisor

**Background:** Elric came to Northreach seeking old ruins and forgotten knowledge. What he found was a region full of mysteries that needed documenting. He joined the Wardens to gain access to their field reports and has stayed because the work keeps surprising him.

**Reputation:** Curious and meticulous. Elric treats every warden's report like a piece of a larger puzzle. He's known for asking follow-up questions that seem irrelevant until they suddenly aren't.

**Common Sayings:**
- "Write it down. Memory fades; ink doesn't."
- "Three reliable witnesses or it didn't happen."

**What People Say About Him:**
- "Elric remembers everything he reads. It's unnerving."
- "He'll ask you about the weather six weeks ago and mean it."
- "The Lorewarden sees patterns where others see coincidence."

## Warden Field Agents

### Corel

**Role:** Trailwarden (veteran tracker and wilderness guide)

**Background:** Corel has spent more time in Northreach's wilderness than in its settlements. Originally from a nomadic trading family, he learned tracking from his parents and expanded his skills through years of hard experience.

**Reputation:** Dependable and laconic. Corel doesn't waste words, and when he speaks about the wilderness, people listen.

**What People Know:**
- Can track anything that leaves a mark
- Prefers sleeping outdoors to sleeping indoors
- Has survived situations that would have killed lesser scouts

---

### Bordel Barleywind

**Role:** Trailwarden (ranger and scout)

**Background:** A halfling who grew up on the frontier, Bordel joined the Wardens after his village was saved by a warden patrol. He sees the guild as a way to pay forward the help his community once received.

**Reputation:** Quiet and observant. Bordel prefers to watch and wait rather than rush in, which has saved his life more than once.

**What People Know:**
- Exceptional at moving unseen
- Uncomfortable in crowds
- Has an uncanny sense for impending danger

---

### Rowan Fairweather

**Role:** Trailwarden (druid and guide)

**Background:** Rowan speaks for the land in a region where most folk see nature as something to survive rather than understand. They joined the Wardens to help people navigate the frontier without destroying it.

**Reputation:** Calm and practical. Rowan offers advice in terms of natural balance—not preachy, but persistent.

**What People Know:**
- Can read weather patterns with unsettling accuracy
- Treats injured animals with the same care as injured people
- Speaks to animals (though most folk assume it's metaphorical)

---

### Mila Teno

**Role:** Trailwarden (rookie scout)

**Background:** Fresh from her training, Mila is eager to prove herself. She volunteered for every patrol until the guild leadership told her to pace herself.

**Reputation:** Enthusiastic and green. Mila's energy is both an asset and a liability—she learns fast but sometimes rushes ahead.

**What People Know:**
- Volunteered for the Glassblades (Uthodurn's militia) before joining the Wardens
- Tries very hard to be taken seriously
- Has yet to face something truly dangerous alone

---

### Takk Oaksplitter

**Role:** Lantern Guard (front-line fighter)

**Background:** A half-orc who earned his name by splitting a tree trunk with a single axe blow during a logging competition. Takk found his calling with the Wardens after years of drifting between jobs.

**Reputation:** Loud, loyal, and quick to laugh. Takk is usually the first to volunteer for dangerous work and the last to leave a fight.

**What People Know:**
- Strong enough to carry an injured person for miles
- Never met a problem that couldn't be solved with direct action
- Terrible at cards but plays anyway

---

### Galvena Aballon

**Role:** Lantern Guard (disciplined protector)

**Background:** Former city guard from a southern settlement, Galvena came north seeking a fresh start after her city fell to political corruption. She believes in order, structure, and protecting those who cannot protect themselves.

**Reputation:** Serious and steadfast. Galvena takes her oaths literally and holds others to their word.

**What People Know:**
- Practices with her weapons every morning
- Doesn't drink on duty (which is most of the time)
- Will stand between danger and innocents without hesitation

---

### Ariodh Highwhirl

**Role:** Lantern Guard (monk and observer)

**Background:** A refugee from distant lands, Ariodh found work with the Wardens after proving his skills in an impromptu tavern brawl (that he didn't start but did finish). He rarely discusses his past.

**Reputation:** Quiet and watchful. Ariodh listens more than he speaks and acts with precision when violence is necessary.

**What People Know:**
- Fights without weapons and wins
- Notices details others miss
- Meditates in the coldest part of the morning

---

### Guz

**Role:** Lantern Guard (bruiser with a past)

**Background:** Guz doesn't talk about where he came from, and his accent suggests somewhere far south. He's trying to live better than he used to, and the Wardens gave him that chance.

**Reputation:** Gruff and blunt. Guz has a short temper but an ironclad code—he protects the innocent, even when it costs him.

**What People Know:**
- Has old scars that tell old stories
- Doesn't lie (even when it would be easier)
- Respects strength but despises bullies

## Magical Specialists

### Sera Gelanadel

**Role:** Lorewatch (apprentice wizard)

**Background:** A young half-elf studying arcane theory under Lorewarden Elric. Sera joined the Wardens to gain practical experience with magic in the field.

**Reputation:** Curious and bookish. Sera asks endless questions and takes copious notes.

**What People Know:**
- Reads everything she can find about Aeorian history
- Nervous around actual danger
- Treats spellcasting like a science experiment

---

### Aurixean Valignaak

**Role:** Lorewatch (sorcerer)

**Background:** A brass dragonborn whose magical talents manifested during puberty. Aurixean joined the Wardens for "adventure, glory, and a chance to prove draconic superiority"—their words.

**Reputation:** Dramatic and confident. Aurixean treats every mission like a performance and insists on being in the front.

**What People Know:**
- Breathes lightning when angry (or excited, or bored)
- Refers to themself in the third person sometimes
- Actually skilled despite the theatrics

---

### Finethir Shinebright

**Role:** Lorewatch (eccentric wizard)

**Background:** A gnome wizard of considerable power and questionable judgment. Shinebright has a reputation for both brilliance and chaos—often simultaneously.

**Reputation:** Brilliant but unpredictable. People respect his magical skill while being wary of his experiments.

**What People Know:**
- Has been seen talking to inanimate objects
- Owns a tower west of the Waystone Inn (locals avoid it)
- Once turned a bandit into a sheep (the bandit reportedly deserved it)

---

### Baleth Cindermoon

**Role:** Lorewatch (charming warlock)

**Background:** An elf with an easy smile and magic that doesn't quite feel like normal spellcasting. Baleth claims his powers come from "a mutually beneficial arrangement" and doesn't elaborate.

**Reputation:** Charming and evasive. Baleth deflects questions about his patron with jokes and subject changes.

**What People Know:**
- Never seems to run out of magical energy
- Always knows more than he's saying
- People trust him despite not quite trusting him

## Healers and Support

### Father Johan Merriksonn

**Role:** Hearthwarden (priest and counselor)

**Background:** A cleric who serves the frontier communities by offering healing and spiritual guidance. Johan believes that compassion is the highest calling.

**Reputation:** Kind and patient. Johan listens without judgment and offers help without strings attached.

**What People Know:**
- Will heal anyone who asks, regardless of their ability to pay
- Keeps confessions absolutely private
- Has seen terrible things but remains gentle

---

### Willen Featherock

**Role:** Hearthwarden (shepherd and survivor)

**Background:** A former shepherd whose flock was attacked by something he couldn't explain. Willen survived and joined the Wardens to help others avoid his fate.

**Reputation:** Nervous but earnest. Willen struggles with confidence but pushes through fear when others need help.

**What People Know:**
- Still has nightmares about what attacked his sheep
- Surprisingly good at first aid
- Tries harder than anyone to be brave

---

### Flynt Wymblen

**Role:** Hearthwarden (bard and storyteller)

**Background:** A traveling bard who joined the Wardens after spending a winter at the Waystone Inn. Flynt realized he preferred stories he could participate in to stories he merely observed.

**Reputation:** Cheerful and chatty. Flynt collects rumors, songs, and stories like some people collect coins.

**What People Know:**
- Knows a song for every occasion
- Pays for information with entertainment
- Turns warden contracts into ballads (with varying accuracy)

---

### Joel Andersmith

**Role:** Hearthwarden (allied farmhand)

**Background:** A local farmer's son who helps the Wardens when they need extra hands. Joel isn't officially a warden but is trusted by the guild.

**Reputation:** Friendly and dependable. Joel represents the community members who support the Wardens' work.

**What People Know:**
- Strong from years of farm work
- Knows everyone in the region
- Will drop everything to help a neighbor

## Local Leaders and Notable Residents

### Elro Aldataur (Palebank Village)

**Role:** Village leader and retired ranger

**Background:** A wood elf who helped found Palebank Village decades ago. Elro still watches over the community with the same vigilance he once used in the wilderness.

**Reputation:** Weathered and wise. Elro has seen winters that would break lesser folk and leads with quiet authority.

**What People Know:**
- Led the defense of Palebank during multiple crises
- Knows every resident by name
- Trusts his instincts about trouble

---

### Pelc (Palebank Village)

**Role:** Merchant and collector

**Background:** An introverted elf who runs Pelc's Curiosities, trading in artifacts and relics from Eiselcross. Pelc values objects more than social interaction.

**Reputation:** Reclusive but knowledgeable. Pelc can identify almost any artifact that passes through their shop.

**What People Know:**
- Opens the shop on their own schedule
- Will pay fair prices for genuine relics
- Guards their privacy fiercely

---

### Tulgi Lutan (Palebank Village)

**Role:** Trapper

**Background:** A mountain dwarf who makes a living trapping in the wilderness around Palebank. Tulgi is known for her independence and her refusal to rely on others.

**Reputation:** Gruff and self-sufficient. Tulgi doesn't ask for help and doesn't offer it unless pressed.

**What People Know:**
- One of the best trappers in the region
- Keeps to herself
- Has a sister named Hulil (rarely mentioned)

---

### Urgon Wenth (Palebank Village)

**Role:** Explorer (deceased)

**Background:** A dwarf who spent a year exploring the ruins of Eiselcross before returning to Palebank Village. Urgon brought back several artifacts from his expedition.

**Reputation (Posthumous):** Brave and ambitious. Locals remember Urgon as someone who took risks to provide for the community.

**What People Remember:**
- Returned from Eiselcross with genuine Aeorian relics
- Fell ill shortly after returning home
- Died in a way that frightened the entire village

---

### Tillus Merrion (Welton)

**Role:** Village council member

**Background:** A respected farmer who serves on Welton's council. Tillus speaks for the agricultural community and advocates for practical solutions.

**Reputation:** Pragmatic and direct. Tillus represents the no-nonsense attitude common in frontier farming communities.

**What People Know:**
- Has farmed the same land for thirty years
- Doesn't believe in magic until it affects his crops
- Takes village safety seriously

---

### Leanor Slatebeard (Welton)

**Role:** Tracker and hunter

**Background:** A dwarf who tracks predators for the village and keeps watch on animal movements near farmland. Leanor knows the wilderness around Welton better than anyone.

**Reputation:** Skilled and cautious. Leanor doesn't take unnecessary risks and teaches others to do the same.

**What People Know:**
- Can track wolves through rain and snow
- Lost a brother to a winter bear years ago
- Warns people about dangers they don't take seriously enough

---

### Westly (Westly's Farm)

**Role:** Shepherd

**Background:** A human shepherd whose farm sits on the western edge of Welton's territory. Westly's livelihood depends on protecting his flock from predators.

**Reputation:** Hardworking and isolated. Westly manages his farm with minimal help and deals with problems on his own.

**What People Know:**
- Raises sheep and goats
- Farms in one of the more dangerous areas

## Cultural Figures and Legends

### The Dragonknights (Historical)

**Role:** Ancient order of warriors

**Background:** Little is known for certain about the Dragonknights beyond their temple in the northwest mountains. Local stories credit them with protecting the region from great evils in ages past.

**Reputation (Legendary):** Heroic and mysterious. The Dragonknights represent an ideal that may or may not have existed as people remember it.

**What Stories Say:**
- They fought alongside dragons
- They sealed something terrible beneath their temple
- Their spirits may still guard the mountain passes

---

### The First Settlers

**Role:** Founders of Northreach communities

**Background:** The first humans, dwarves, and elves who settled in Northreach generations ago. Their descendants still live in the region.

**Reputation (Historical):** Tough and determined. The first settlers are remembered for surviving conditions that would have driven others away.

**What Stories Say:**
- They came north seeking freedom from southern wars
- They made pacts with local communities already living here
- They learned the hard way which places to avoid

## Types of People You'll Meet in Northreach

### Homesteaders
Farmers, shepherds, and small-holders who work the land. They're practical folk who measure time in seasons and success in harvests.

### Trappers and Hunters
People who make their living from the wilderness. They know the land's dangers intimately and respect the balance between predator and prey.

### Traders and Merchants
Those who move goods between settlements. They bring news along with supplies and know the value of everything they carry.

### Wanderers and Drifters
People passing through—some seeking opportunities, others fleeing pasts. Northreach asks few questions about where you've been.

### Seekers and Scholars
Those drawn to Northreach by its mysteries and ruins. They're usually more interested in ancient history than current problems.

### Wardens and Guards
\page

Professional protectors who handle the dangers that ordinary folk can't. They earn their reputation through actions, not titles.

## Social Customs and Etiquette

### Hospitality
- Travelers are offered food and shelter, but are expected to contribute (labor, coin, or trade goods)
- Refusing hospitality is considered suspicious
- Overstaying welcome is remembered

### Introductions
- Names and current business matter; past history is your own
- A firm handshake and direct eye contact show honesty
- Avoiding questions is respected more than lying

### Community Obligations
- Everyone contributes during planting and harvest
- When someone calls for help, you respond
- Debts are remembered for years

### Warnings and Advice
- When a local warns you about something, listen
- Ignoring safety advice marks you as foolish
- Veteran survivors are respected regardless of age or background

## Reputation in Northreach

In a frontier region, your reputation is built through actions:

**Good Reputation Comes From:**
- Keeping your word
- Helping without being asked
- Sharing important warnings
- Paying your debts
- Standing your ground when it matters

**Bad Reputation Comes From:**
- Breaking contracts
- Abandoning people in danger
- Stealing from community resources
- Disrespecting local customs
- Running from reasonable fights

**Reputation is Local:** Someone might be a hero in Welton and unknown in Palebank. Word travels, but slowly.
