# World Building Directory

This directory contains lore, world-building materials, and reference documents for the **Northwatch Wardens** campaign setting.

---

## Directory Structure

### **PlayerFacing/**
Lore information that players can freely access about Northreach and the campaign setting. These documents are written from an in-world perspective and contain no spoilers.

**Contents:**
- `People_of_Northreach.md` - NPCs, factions, cultural figures, and social customs
- `Places_of_Northreach.md` - Locations, geography, and settlements throughout the region
- `Cultures_of_Northreach.md` - Different peoples, traditions, and the frontier culture

**Purpose:** 
Use these as handouts or references for players to deepen their understanding of the setting. Everything here is safe for player eyes.

---

### **DMEyesOnly/**
Secret information for the DM including hidden motivations, conspiracy details, and major campaign spoilers. **Never share with players unless discovered through gameplay.**

**Contents:**
- `People_Secrets.md` - Hidden NPC backgrounds, motivations, and connections to the Aeorian Echo
- `Places_Secrets.md` - Secret features of locations, dark histories, and hidden threats
- `The_Aeorian_Echo.md` - Complete master document of the campaign's central threat and plotline

**Purpose:**
These documents provide the DM with the full picture of what's really happening in Northreach, allowing for consistent foreshadowing and dramatic reveals.

---

### **Drafts/**
Work-in-progress materials, archived notes, and development documents. These may contain incomplete or experimental content.

**Structure:**
- `Locations_South/` - Southern regions beyond current campaign scope
- `Religion/` - Pantheon and religious system development
- `Setting/` - General setting notes and off-map world lore
- `Transcripts/` - Development discussions and ideation records

**Purpose:**
Preserve development history and maintain notes for future expansion while keeping active content organized.

## Content Guidelines

### PlayerFacing Documents Should:
- Be written from an in-world perspective
- Contain common knowledge, rumors, and observable information
- Avoid spoilers about hidden plots or secret motivations
- Include atmospheric details that enhance immersion
- Present information as characters would learn it

### DMEyesOnly Documents Should:
- Contain the "truth" behind player-facing information
- Explain hidden motivations and secret connections
- Detail the mechanics of threats and mysteries
- Provide multiple options for DM flexibility
- Include guidance on revelation timing

### Drafts Should:
- Preserve work-in-progress for future development
- Document decisions made during development
- Store experimental content not yet ready for active use
- Maintain historical record of setting evolution

## Campaign Integration

### The Aeorian Echo Plotline

The central threat of Northwatch Wardens Season One involves the reactivation of ancient Aeorian facilities scattered across the region. This conspiracy operates on multiple levels:

**What Players Know:**
- Strange occurrences are happening (wolves, illnesses, thefts)
- Something connects these events
- The region faces an escalating threat

**What the DM Knows:**
- A surviving Aeorian intelligence is systematically reactivating ancient weapons
- Multiple NPCs have hidden connections to this threat
- The players can discover the truth through investigation
- The campaign builds to confronting the source of the Echo

**Revelation Structure:**
1. **Early:** Local mysteries seem unconnected
2. **Mid:** Patterns emerge, Aeorian origin discovered
3. **Late:** Full scope revealed, final confrontation prepared

## Thematic Elements

### Core Themes
- **Frontier Survival:** Community and mutual aid matter

- **Hidden Truths:** Investigation reveals larger patterns

- Practical frontier realism with creeping dread
- Mystery that builds to revelation
- Hope despite overwhelming odds
- Earned heroism through dedication

### Atmosphere
- Wide wilderness and isolated settlements
- Cold, unforgiving environment
- Strange occurrences at the edge of awareness
- Growing sense that something is wrong

## Quick Reference

### Starting a Session
1. Review relevant PlayerFacing documents for location/NPCs
2. Check DMEyesOnly secrets for characters involved
3. Consider what players might discover
4. Prepare to improvise within established canon

### Player Questions
- **About setting:** Reference PlayerFacing documents
- **About NPCs:** Share public persona, hide secrets
- **About past events:** Provide in-world knowledge level
- **About mysteries:** Give clues, not answers

### Revelation Moments
- Build suspense before big reveals
- Let players piece things together
- Reward investigation and cleverness
- Some secrets discovered dramatically, others gradually

## Contributing to World Building

### Adding New Material

**For Player Content:**
1. Ensure it fits established tone and setting
2. Write from in-world perspective
3. Avoid unintentional spoilers
4. Place in appropriate PlayerFacing file

**For DM Content:**
1. Connect to established plotlines
2. Consider revelation timing
3. Maintain consistency with existing secrets
4. Place in appropriate DMEyesOnly file

**For Draft Material:**
1. Mark clearly as work-in-progress
2. Note any conflicts with canon
3. Store in appropriate Drafts subfolder
4. Update Scratchpad.md index

### Maintaining Consistency
- Cross-reference new content with existing documents
- Update all affected documents when making changes
- Preserve player-DM information separation
- Document major decisions in Drafts/Transcripts
