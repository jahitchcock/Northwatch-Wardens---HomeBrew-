# The Stonebound Depths — The Dwarven Kingdom

**Theme:** Geomancy, resonance, deep-earth magic  
**Tone:** Monumental, echoing, ancient  
**Role in the World:** The unconscious witness; built around a dormant artifact core

---

## Overview

Deep beneath the mountains lies the realm of the Stonebound Dwarves, a people who believe the world's oldest truths are written in stone. They have built their entire civilization around massive crystalline formations and strange metallic structures they call "Deep Roots"—treating them as natural geological features rather than what they truly are.

The dwarves are master craftsmen, resonance-mages, and geomancers. Their magic developed from studying the subtle vibrations of the very artifacts they live among. They record tremors, interpret stone-songs, and forge with harmonic precision, never realizing they're dancing to the rhythm of dormant Aeorian technology.

Eclipse Day sent shockwaves through the Depths. The Heartstone—a massive artifact core they worship as a natural wonder—hummed for the first time in recorded history. The dwarves believe a new "verse" in the world's song has begun. They have no idea their sacred geological marvel is awakening.

## Geography

### Location
**Position:** Mountain range between Northreach and Solaris  
**Climate:** Subterranean (temperature stable, humid in lower depths)  
**Terrain:** Massive cavern systems, underground rivers, mineral veins  
**Access:** Surface entry points well-guarded; most of kingdom underground

### The Mountain Range
**Surface Features:**
- Tall, ancient peaks
- Volcanic origins (mostly dormant)
- Rich mineral deposits
- Sparse surface settlements (mining outposts)
- Dangerous terrain

**Underground Expanse:**
- Hundreds of miles of worked caverns
- Natural cave systems enhanced by mining
- Underground rivers and lakes
- Geothermal vents
- Massive chambers (some natural, some carved)
- Interconnected levels spanning vertical miles


### Levels of the Depths

**Upper Reaches** (0-1000 feet)
- Trading posts and surface interface
- Fresh air circulation
- Lighter industry
- Mushroom farms
- Connection to outside world

**Middle Halls** (1000-3000 feet)
- Primary residential areas
- Craft districts and forges
- Markets and guildhalls
- Government buildings
- Most of population lives here

**Lower Veins** (3000-5000 feet)
- Deep mining operations
- Rare mineral extraction
- Geothermal forges
- Dangerous but profitable
- Elite miners only

**The Foundations** (5000+ feet)
- Ancient levels, partially sealed
- Heartstone Chamber location
- Rune-Singers' Hall
- Deep-Readers' Archive
- Restricted areas
- Where "Deep Roots" are strongest

**The Forgotten Deeps** (Deepest levels)
- Sealed and abandoned
- Dangerous tremors
- Strange echoes
- Ancient excavations
- Sometimes things are found here
- Sometimes things are lost here

## Khardûn-Tharum, the Deep Crown

### Overview
The capital city, a monumental underground metropolis carved around a massive crystalline structure the dwarves call the **Heartstone**.

**Population:** ~45,000 (overwhelmingly dwarven)  
**Age:** Over 3,000 years (on this site)  
**Architecture:** Carved stone, precise geometric patterns, resonant design  
**Atmosphere:** Echoing, monumental, ancient


### The Heartstone
The centerpiece of dwarven civilization:

**Physical Appearance:**
- Massive crystalline structure
- 200 feet tall, 80 feet wide at base
- Translucent pale blue with metallic veins
- Geometric facets
- Internal glow (faint, steady... usually)
- Hums at inaudible frequencies

**What Dwarves Believe:**
- Natural geological formation
- Crystallized essence of the mountain
- Heart of the stone's song
- Sacred but not supernatural
- Stable, eternal, trustworthy
- Foundation of their civilization

**What It Actually Is:**
- Dormant Aeorian artifact core
- Power distribution node for ancient network
- Incredibly advanced technology
- Thousands of years inactive
- Now beginning to wake
- The dwarves built their capital around it without knowing

**Current State:**
- Since Eclipse Day, occasionally hums audibly
- Crystals vibrate
- Internal light pulses
- Runes shift
- Patterns form in its facets
- Dwarves excited, not alarmed
- Interpreting as natural evolution

### Major Districts

**The Heartstone Chamber**
Cathedral-like cavern housing the core:
- Treated as sacred geological site
- Public viewing areas
- Research platforms built around it
- Guard posts (protecting it from damage)
- Meditation spaces
- Where Deep-Readers interpret vibrations
- Now the busiest place in the city


**The Resonant Forges**
Workshops built around harmonic metal veins:
- Metalworking using sound and vibration
- Forging produces subtle enchantments
- Master crafters create resonant weapons
- Armor that "sings" when struck
- Tools that improve with use
- Dwarven metalwork renowned worldwide

**The Rune-Singers' Hall**
Amphitheater where magic is shaped through sound:
- Circular chamber with perfect acoustics
- Runes carved in concentric rings
- Magic channeled through voice and percussion
- Resonance creates effects
- Training ground for rune-singers
- Public performances and ceremonies

**The Deep-Readers' Archive**
Geological records carved into stone tablets:
- Thousands of years of seismic data
- Tremor patterns and interpretations
- "Messages from the deep"
- Prophecies read in stone-song
- Currently in chaos: new patterns don't match records
- Readers working frantically to interpret

**The Lower Veins**
Mining districts for rare crystals and metals:
- Dangerous work
- High pay
- Elite guilds
- Competition fierce
- Growth accelerating near Heartstone
- Some miners reporting strange finds

**The Council Halls**
Government seat for Stonebound Council:
- Master artisans
- Guild leaders
- Geomancers
- Deep-Readers
- Military commanders
- Currently in continuous session (Eclipse Day response)

## Culture and Society

### The Stone Philosophy

**Core Beliefs:**
- Stone is scripture
- Mountains remember
- Everything is geology (even magic)
- Patience and precision are virtues
- What is built well lasts forever
- The deep has secrets, but they're natural secrets

**In Practice:**
- Craftsmanship as spiritual practice
- Long-term thinking (centuries, not years)
- Respect for natural world (as they understand it)
- Magic as science (resonance, vibration)
- Distrust of things that won't last
- Everything must be built to endure

### Social Structure

**The Stonebound Council**
Governing body, meritocratic selection:
- **Forgemaster Grimnar Ironvein** — Master smith, traditionalist
- **Rune-Singer Thalia Deepchant** — Magical authority, open-minded
- **Deep-Reader Borin Stonescript** — Chief interpreter, troubled by new data
- **Mining Guildmaster Kara Gemcutter** — Resource management, pragmatic
- **Commander Thorek Shieldbreaker** — Military, security-focused

**Guild System:**
- Forgers' Guild (smithing and metalwork)
- Miners' Guild (extraction and prospecting)
- Rune-Singers' Guild (magical practitioners)
- Deep-Readers' Guild (geological interpreters)
- Builders' Guild (architecture and engineering)
- Brewers' Guild (essential!)

Each guild internally ranked by skill:
- Apprentice
- Journeyman
- Master
- Grandmaster (rare, legendary)

**Family Clans:**
- Lineage matters but doesn't guarantee position
- Clans have reputations and specialties
- Clan rivalries friendly (mostly)
- Inter-clan marriages common
- Foundlings adopted into clans readily


**Class Mobility:**
Relatively high:
- Skill determines rank
- Apprentices from any background
- Wealth helps but doesn't define
- Master craftsman respected regardless of origin
- But: upper guilds have advantages

### Daily Life

**Work:**
- 12-hour days standard (no sun means flexible scheduling)
- Shift work in forges and mines
- Apprentices work longer hours
- Masters set own hours
- Rest days regular
- Pride in work universal

**Food:**
- Mushroom-based cuisine
- Underground fish from rivers
- Imported grains and vegetables (expensive)
- Beer (made from mushroom malt)
- Cheese (from surface trade)
- Hearty, filling, practical

**Entertainment:**
- Singing (resonant harmonies)
- Stone-carving competitions
- Wrestling and strength contests
- Storytelling (historical, geological)
- Drinking (competitive and social)
- Rhythmic drumming

**Family:**
- Large families valued
- Children raised communally by clan
- Elderly respected as repositories of knowledge
- Homes multi-generational
- Strong community bonds

**Values:**
- Craftsmanship
- Honesty (stone doesn't lie)
- Loyalty (to clan, guild, city)
- Endurance
- Practicality
- Tradition (but flexible tradition)

## Magic in the Depths

### Resonance Magic
The distinctive dwarven tradition:

**Theory:**
- Everything vibrates at specific frequencies
- Stone carries memory in its structure
- Sound can shape reality
- Runes focus vibration
- Harmony creates effects
- Dissonance destroys

**Practice:**
- Voice as primary tool (rune-singing)
- Hammers and chisels as instruments
- Rhythmic work as spell-casting
- Forging incorporates magic naturally
- Architecture designed for resonance
- Living in constant subtle magic

**Applications:**
- **Structural Magic:** Buildings that repair themselves
- **Forge-Craft:** Enchantments built into items during creation
- **Seismic Sensing:** Reading tremors and stone-song
- **Defensive Wards:** Vibrations that repel or detect
- **Communication:** Stone carries messages (slowly)
- **Historical Reading:** Extracting memory from stone

### Rune-Singing
The formal magical tradition:

**Training:**
- Decades of study
- Perfect pitch required
- Knowledge of rune meanings
- Physical endurance (singing is exhausting)
- Understanding of geology
- Apprenticeship under master

**Techniques:**
- Harmonic chanting
- Runic percussion
- Resonant visualization
- Group work (multiple singers in harmony)
- Solo performances for precision
- Emergency fast-singing (dangerous, imprecise)


**Common Spells:**
- Shaping stone (gradual, permanent)
- Detecting minerals
- Strengthening structures
- Creating light (phosphorescent resonance)
- Defensive barriers
- Seismic detection

### Deep-Reading
Interpretation of geological phenomena:

**What They Read:**
- Seismic tremors (location, pattern, intensity)
- Crystal formation rates
- Metal vein growth
- Underground water flow
- Temperature shifts
- Heartstone vibrations

**How They Interpret:**
- Historical precedent
- Pattern matching
- Mathematical analysis
- Intuition (experienced readers)
- Debate and consensus
- Recording everything

**What It Means (To Them):**
- Prophecy (the mountain predicts)
- Warning (earthquake risk)
- Opportunity (new veins forming)
- Blessing (stable periods)
- Mystery (unexplained patterns)

**What It Actually Is:**
They're reading Aeorian artifact network activity:
- Tremors are node activations
- Crystal growth is energy discharge
- Patterns match network communications
- Their entire magical tradition developed from listening to dormant technology
- They've been unconscious witnesses for millennia
- Now the technology is waking up

## Eclipse Day in the Depths

### The Event
When Eclipse Day struck the surface, the Depths felt it immediately:

**Physical Effects:**
- Massive tremor (strongest in 500 years)
- Heartstone hummed audibly for first time ever
- Crystals throughout city vibrated
- Some stone cracked in geometric patterns
- Metal veins glowed briefly
- Underground rivers churned


**Magical Effects:**
- Rune-singers experienced power surge
- Some spells doubled in effectiveness
- Others failed completely
- New resonances emerged
- Old wards flickered
- Something felt different

**Social Response:**
- Emergency lockdown initially
- Damage assessment
- Deep-Readers mobilized
- Council convened
- Public panic (controlled)
- Then: excitement

**The Interpretation:**
Deep-Readers declared: "A new verse in the world's song has begun."

Meaning (to dwarves):
- Geological shift (natural)
- New era (positive)
- Opportunity (more minerals will form)
- Prophecy fulfilled (debatable)
- Requires study but not fear

**Why They're Wrong:**
- Not geological: technological
- Not new verse: awakening alarm
- Not opportunity: warning
- Not natural evolution: deliberate reactivation
- They're reading machine language as poetry

### Ongoing Effects
Months later:

**The Heartstone:**
- Hums audibly daily now
- Internal light pulses
- Patterns form and shift in facets
- Deep-Readers working constantly to interpret
- Some patterns match ancient records exactly
- Others completely new
- Growing concern but also excitement

**Seismic Activity:**
- Increased tremors throughout region
- New patterns emerging
- Crystal growth accelerated
- Metal veins expanding
- Some mines becoming too dangerous
- But also: unprecedented finds


**Social Impact:**
- Increased mining activity
- Research into Heartstone intensifying
- Foreign scholars requesting access
- Religious interpretations emerging
- Some dwarves uneasy
- Most excited about discoveries

**The Divided Response:**
- **Traditionalists:** Trust the records; this has happened before
- **Progressives:** New phenomenon; we need outside help
- **Opportunists:** Profit from increased mining
- **Worried:** Something feels wrong about this

## Secrets and Revelations

### For the DM

**The Core Truth:**
- Heartstone is Aeorian artifact core
- Entire city built around it unknowingly
- Dwarven magic tradition developed from studying its emanations
- All seismic readings are actually artifact network traffic
- Eclipse Day was network-wide activation pulse
- Heartstone is waking up
- Eventually it will activate fully
- Consequences: catastrophic or transformative depending on response

**Specific Secrets:**
- **Original Builders:** Dwarves found this site, didn't create it
- **First Excavations:** Uncovered Aeorian structures, mistook for natural
- **Ancient Records:** Some Deep-Readers' archives contain truth, not recognized
- **Sealed Levels:** Contain more Aeorian technology
- **Forgotten Deeps:** Things trapped down there, now stirring
- **Network Connection:** Heartstone linked to Salsvault and all other nodes

**NPC Secrets:**
- **Deep-Reader Borin Stonescript:** Increasingly suspects truth; frightened
- **Rune-Singer Thalia Deepchant:** Spells working differently; investigating why
- **Forgemaster Grimnar Ironvein:** Found ancient non-dwarven tool; hiding it
- **Commander Thorek Shieldbreaker:** Military prepared for structural failure
- **Kara Gemcutter:** Miners reporting constructs in deepest veins

**The Vibration Code:**
Heartstone pulses encode information:
- Instructions in Aeorian language
- Network status reports
- Activation sequences
- If decoded: crucial intelligence
- But: decoding would reveal truth
- Truth might cause cultural collapse


### Connection to Campaign

**Early (Northreach-focused):**
- Dwarven metalwork found in Northreach
- Occasional dwarven NPC
- Rumors of earthquakes in mountains
- Distant concern

**Mid (Regional awareness):**
- Party learns dwarves experiencing similar phenomena
- Seismic patterns correlate with Salsvault activity

**Late (Continental scope):**
- Dwarves realize truth about Heartstone
- Cultural crisis as foundation of beliefs shaken

- Or might amplify it

**Epic (Final resolution):**
- Heartstone could be weapon, shield, or bomb
- Dwarven rune-singers essential to solution
- But: using Heartstone risks entire city
- Moral choice: sacrifice dwarven capital to save world?
- Or: find way to deactivate without destroying?
- Resonance magic vs. Aeorian technology
