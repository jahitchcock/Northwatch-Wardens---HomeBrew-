## Travel Times

### From Waystone Inn (Guild Headquarters)

| Destination | Distance | Travel Time | Road Quality |
|:------------|:---------|:------------|:-------------|
| **Welton** (Southwest) | ~30 miles | 1–2 days | Fair; rural road |
| **Westly's Farm** (via Welton) | ~35 miles | 2–3 days | Poor; farm track |
| **Pinebrook** (Southeast) | ~45 miles | 2–3 days | Fair; trade route |
| **Noke's Tower** (West) | ~8 miles | ½–1 day | Good; river path |
| **Palebank Village** (Northeast) | ~60 miles | 3–5 days | Poor; coastal trail |
| **Croaker Cave** (North of Palebank) | ~70 miles | 4–6 days | Poor; wilderness |
| **Temple of the Dragonknights** (Northwest) | ~80 miles | 5–7 days | Poor; mountain trail |
| **Salsvault Ruins** (Far North) | ~120 miles | 8–12 days | Treacherous; ice fields |

### Travel Modifiers

**Weather Conditions:**
- **Clear Weather:** Standard travel times
- **Light Rain/Snow:** +1 day per 3 days of travel
- **Heavy Weather:** +1 day per day of travel; may require shelter
- **Winter Storms:** Travel may become impossible; seek shelter immediately

**Party Composition:**
- **Small Party (2–4):** Standard travel times
- **Large Party (5+):** +10% travel time
- **Mounted Party:** –20% travel time on good roads
- **With Wagons:** +50% travel time; poor roads may be impassable

### Waystone Inn (Warden Headquarters)

**Warden Members:** Modest lodging and meals provided at **no cost** while on active contracts

**Non-Members:**
- **Lodging:** 5 sp/night (modest room)
- **Meals:** 3 sp (modest meal), 5 sp (comfortable meal)
- **Stabling:** 5 cp/night per mount
- **Wagon Storage:** 1 sp/night

**Atmosphere:** Professional, warm, neutral ground

\column

### The Shepherd's Crook Inn (Welton)

**Standard Pricing:**
- **Lodging:** 4 sp/night (modest room)
- **Meals:** 2 sp (modest meal)
- **Stabling:** 3 cp/night per mount

**Special Notes:**
- Prices rise when rumors of danger spread (+50% during emergencies)
- Good source of local gossip and news
- Popular with farmers and traders

### Pinebrook Trade Post

**Standard Pricing:**
- **Lodging:** 6 sp/night (modest to comfortable)
- **Meals:** 3–4 sp (modest meal)
- **Stabling:** 5 cp/night per mount

**Special Notes:**
- Trading hub; slightly higher prices
- Better selection of goods than Welton
- More transient population

### Palebank Village

**Standard Pricing:**
- **Lodging:** 4 sp/night (poor to modest)
- **Meals:** 2 sp (modest meal, fish-heavy)
- **Stabling:** Limited (most use fishing boats)

**Special Notes:**
- Coastal settlement; fresh seafood
- Cold and isolated; fewer amenities
- Prices can spike during bad weather

## Spellcasting Services

### Availability in Northreach

**Magic is rare in the frontier.** Most settlements have no resident spellcasters. The Northwatch Wardens maintain the most reliable access to magical services in the region.

### Waystone Inn (Warden Resources)

**Lorewarden Elric Vael** can provide limited spellcasting services to active Wardens:

| Spell | Level | Cost | Availability |
|:------|:------|:-----|:-------------|
| **Cure Wounds** | 1st | Free (Wardens) | Common |
| **Identify** | 1st | Free (Wardens) | Common |
| **Detect Magic** | 1st | Free (Wardens) | Common |
| **Lesser Restoration** | 2nd | 20 gp | Uncommon |
| **Remove Curse** | 3rd | 50 gp | Rare |
| **Greater Restoration** | 5th | 200 gp | Very Rare |
| **Raise Dead** | 5th | 500 gp + diamonds | Extremely Rare |

**Notes:**
- Non-Wardens pay double
- Higher-level spells may require special request
- Elric prioritizes contract-related needs

### Temple Services (Welton, Pinebrook)

**Small frontier shrines** may offer basic healing:

| Service | Cost | Notes |
|:--------|:-----|:------|
| **Cure Wounds** | 10 gp | If shrine priest available |
| **Lesser Restoration** | 40 gp | Rare; traveling priests only |
| **Blessing/Guidance** | Donation | Common; suggested 1 sp–1 gp |

**Availability:** Inconsistent; frontier has few resident clerics

### Black Market (Pinebrook, Major Settlements)

**Illegal or gray-market magical services** exist but carry risk:

- Prices negotiable (often inflated)
- Quality uncertain
- May attract unwanted attention
- DM discretion on availability

## Frontier Conditions

### Northreach Modifiers

**The frontier is not the city.** Prices, availability, and services differ from core D&D assumptions.

### Price Adjustments

| Item Category | Modifier | Reason |
|:--------------|:---------|:-------|
| **Basic Supplies** | Standard | Locally produced |
| **Imported Goods** | +50% | Long supply chains |
| **Luxury Items** | +100% | Rare; high demand |
| **Magic Items** | +200% | Extremely scarce |
| **Cold Weather Gear** | Standard | Locally essential |
| **Emergency Supplies** | +100–300% | Demand spikes during crisis |

### Availability Guide

**Common:** Available in most settlements (Waystone Inn, Welton, Pinebrook)

**Uncommon:** Available at larger settlements or by special order (1–3 days)

**Rare:** Only at Waystone Inn or major trade hubs; may require travel to larger cities

**Very Rare:** Special order; weeks or months to acquire

**Legendary:** Not available in Northreach; requires expedition to distant cities

### Seasonal Variations

#### Winter (6 months: Late Fall through Early Spring)

- **Travel Times:** +50% due to snow and ice
- **Food Prices:** +25% (limited fresh produce)
- **Cold Weather Gear:** Essential for survival
- **Road Conditions:** Poor to impassable
- **Services:** Limited; many craftsmen stay indoors

#### Summer (4 months: Late Spring through Early Fall)

- **Travel Times:** Standard
- **Food Prices:** Standard to –10% (harvest season)
- **Road Conditions:** Fair to good
- **Services:** Widely available

#### Shoulder Seasons (2 months total)

- **Travel:** Variable; mud season can be worse than winter
- **Prices:** Standard
- **Services:** Preparing for next season

### Local Customs & Expectations

#### Frontier Etiquette

**Hospitality:**
- Travelers are offered basic shelter in emergencies
- Repay kindness with labor, coin, or future aid
- Never abuse hospitality; reputation matters

**Trade:**
- Haggling is expected for large purchases
- Fair weights and honest prices are valued
- Cheating or stealing brings harsh consequences

**Neutrality:**
- The Waystone Inn is neutral ground
- Starting fights at the inn is forbidden
- Marshal Thorne enforces this strictly

**Community Aid:**
- Settlers help neighbors survive
- Wardens are expected to assist in emergencies
- Refusing aid to those in need damages reputation

#### Currency Notes

**Standard Exchange Rates:**
- 1 gold piece (gp) = 10 silver pieces (sp)
- 1 silver piece (sp) = 10 copper pieces (cp)
- 1 gold piece (gp) = 100 copper pieces (cp)

**Practical Use in Northreach:**
- **Copper:** Daily expenses (meals, stabling, small goods)
- **Silver:** Lodging, services, local trade
- **Gold:** Major purchases, contracts, wages
- **Platinum:** Rarely seen; exotic transactions only

**Barter is Common:**
- Many frontier folk trade goods directly
- Wardens can often trade service for supplies
- Some settlements prefer trade goods to coin

## Quick Notes for Players

**Always Carry:**
- Rope (50 ft minimum)
- Torches or lantern with oil
- Rations (3 days minimum)
- Waterskin
- Cold weather gear (during cold months)
- Tinderbox

**Before Leaving Waystone Inn:**
- Check contract details
- Confirm travel time and route
- Ensure adequate supplies
- Note nearest settlements for resupply
- Report expected return date

**In the Field:**
- Weather can change rapidly
- Settlements are days apart
- Magic is unreliable
- Wildlife is dangerous
- Trust your instincts

**Emergency Protocol:**
- Return to nearest settlement
- Send word to Waystone Inn
- Request reinforcements if needed
- Wardens support each other
