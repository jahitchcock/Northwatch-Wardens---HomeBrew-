# Glossary of Aevoria

*A quick reference guide to terms, places, organizations, and people of the world*

---

This glossary provides pronunciation guides and brief definitions for important names, places, and concepts in Aevoria. For more detailed information, see the referenced chapters.

**Pronunciation Key:**
- Capitals indicate stressed syllables
- Phonetic spellings use common English sounds
- Regional variations noted where significant

:::

## A

**Aeor** (AY-or)
Ancient civilization of powerful mages that fell in a cataclysm. Ruins of Aeor lie buried in the Far North, and Aeorian artifacts occasionally surface. Known for ambitious arcane experiments and magical hubris. *See: The Far North, Salsvault*

**Aeorian Echo**
Phenomenon of rising magical energy emanating from buried Aeorian ruins, particularly Salsvault. Causes unpredictable magical effects, awakening intelligence in creatures, and destabilizing arcane workings. Central mystery of the Northwatch Wardens campaign. *See: Frozen Sick adventure*

**Aevoria** (ay-VOR-ee-uh)
The continent and world in which the Northwatch Wardens campaign takes place. Divided into ten major regions with diverse cultures, climates, and peoples.

**Archivists of Aeor**
Scholarly organization dedicated to studying Aeorian ruins and recovering lost knowledge safely and responsibly. Based primarily in northern research stations. Viewed with a mix of respect and caution—their work is valuable but dangerous. *See: Organizations chapter*

**Argyle** (AR-gyle)
*Common pronunciation mistake: "ar-GYE-luh"*
Settlement in the Far North. Minor frontier post dealing with harsh arctic conditions.

**Aurixean Valignaak** (or-IK-see-an VAL-ig-nahk)
*Dragon name. Common mistake: "OR-ex-ee-an"*
Silver dragon scholar and Northwatch Warden. Curious, methodical, and somewhat awkward in social situations. Specializes in investigation and historical research. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

## B

**Baleth Cindermoon**
Half-elf Warden from the Emberlands. Quiet, pragmatic survivor with deep knowledge of disaster relief and improvised solutions. Carries scars from the Emberlands' destruction. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Bordel Barleywind**
Halfling chef and keeper of Waystone Inn. Jovial, welcoming, and observant. Provides comfort food, local gossip, and practical wisdom. The Inn's emotional heart. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Brenna Thorne**
Marshal of the Northwatch Wardens. Human fighter from Northreach. Direct, practical leader with military background. Commands field operations and makes tactical decisions. "Work smarter, not just harder." *See: People of Northreach chapter, Northwatch Wardens chapter*

## C

**Cloakwood**
Forest region in Northreach. Dense woodland home to rangers, druids, and occasional dangerous creatures. Trade route passes through its edge.

**Corel**
Wood elf ranger-druid hybrid working with the Northwatch Wardens. Speaks little, prefers action. Expert tracker and wilderness guide. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Covenant of the Silent Vigil**
Religious order dedicated to protecting society from unaccountable magical power. Opposes reckless arcane experimentation, demonic pacts, and dangerous artifacts. Not anti-magic, but pro-responsibility. Views vary by region—respected enforcers or zealous opposition. *See: Organizations chapter, Gods and Religion chapter*

**Croaker Cave**
Bandit hideout north of Palebank Village. Named for the croaking sounds echoing from within (frogs, not bandits). Site of conflict during Frozen Sick adventure. *See: The Frozen Sick adventure*

**Currency**
Aevoria uses standard D&D coinage: copper pieces (cp), silver pieces (sp), gold pieces (gp), and platinum pieces (pp). Regional currencies exist but are easily exchanged. *See: Currency and Trade chapter*

## D

**Divinity's Beacon** (div-IN-ih-tees BEE-kun)
Holy city in the heartlands of the Solaris Dominion. Multi-faith center of pilgrimage and theological study. Neutral ground for all faiths. Home to grand temples, libraries, and religious scholars. Known for hospitality to pilgrims and fierce opposition to unaccountable magic. *See: Places of Legend chapter*

**Dragonknights, The**
Cult of dragon-worshippers who sought to gain draconic power through rituals and pacts. Led by Brother Alastair. Believed dragons were superior beings deserving worship and service. Defeated in the Temple of the Dragonknights adventure. *See: Temple of the Dragonknights adventure*

## E

**Eclipse Day**
Historical event in Solaris. Natural solar eclipse that occurred during a major cultural moment. Commemorated with silence and reflection. Specific details vary by telling. *See: Solaris Dominion chapter*

**Elric Vael** (EL-rik VAYL)
Lorewarden of the Northwatch Wardens. Half-elf scholar and divination wizard. Provides research support, arcane expertise, and historical context. Curious, methodical, slightly absent-minded. "The answer is in the archives." *See: People of Northreach chapter, Northwatch Wardens chapter*

**Elro Aldataur** (EL-roh al-DAH-tor)
*Common mistake: "el-ROH al-duh-TAR"*
Elf scholar in Palebank Village. Researches Aeorian history and the source of the mysterious illness. Knowledgeable but somewhat reckless in pursuit of discovery. *See: People of Northreach chapter, Frozen Sick adventure*

**Emberlands**
Region of Aevoria devastated by magical cataclysm within living memory. Landscape torn by lingering fire and earth magic. Home to resilient survivors, the Red Covenant disaster relief organization, and desperate communities. Known for improvisation and fierce determination. *See: Regions chapter*

## F

**Far North, The**
Arctic region at the top of the world. Home to buried Aeorian ruins, extreme weather, and isolated settlements. Inhabited by hardy folk, nomadic tribes, and researchers studying Aeor. Source of the Aeorian Echo phenomenon. *See: Regions chapter*

**Father Johan Merriksonn** (YOH-han MEH-rik-sun)
*"Father Johan" is fine; full name rarely used*
Dwarf cleric of the Stonefather in Northreach. Provides healing, spiritual guidance, and community support. Practical faith focused on endurance and honest work. Based in a modest shrine near Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Finethir Shinebright** (FIN-uh-theer SHINE-bright)
*Sheep polymorphed into human form*
Polymorphed sheep seeking help to stop his polymorphing wizard brother Noke. Panicked, confused, and desperate. Central character in The Wild Sheep Chase adventure. *See: Wild Sheep Chase adventure*

**First Settlers, The**
Historical group that established Northreach settlements generations ago. Arrived from various southern regions seeking opportunity and freedom. Their stories and traditions form the foundation of Northreach culture. *See: Northreach chapter, Songs of Northreach*

**Freelance Brotherhood**
Loose network of independent adventurers, mercenaries, and problem-solvers. Not a formal organization—more of a mutual aid society. Helps members find work, share information, and avoid dangerous contracts. Viewed as pragmatic professionals or unreliable freelancers depending on perspective. *See: Organizations chapter*

**Frozen Sick**
Mysterious illness affecting Palebank Village. Victims develop icy blue veins, fever despite cold skin, and eventual crystallization. Caused by exposure to Aeorian spores from Salsvault. Curable with frigid woe antidote. *See: Frozen Sick adventure*

**Flynt Wymblen**
Gnome tinkerer and inventor working with the Northwatch Wardens. Enthusiastic gadgeteer with variable success rate. Provides experimental equipment and creative solutions (with occasional explosions). Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

Human ex-soldier recruited to the Northwatch Wardens. Disciplined, loyal, struggles with nightmares from past battles. Competent warrior seeking new purpose. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Gleaners, The**
Rural mutual aid network in farming regions. Help neighbors with harvests, barn-raisings, and recovery from disasters. Informal but reliable. Represent community values of shared labor. *See: Organizations chapter*

**Guz**
Half-orc laborer and Northwatch Warden. Quiet, immensely strong, kind-hearted. Does heavy lifting and manual work. Often underestimated due to his silence. Excellent judge of character. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter, Songs of Northreach*

## N

**Noke** (NOHK)
*Rhymes with "spoke"*
Polymorphing wizard conducting dangerous experiments. Finethir's brother. Accidentally transformed himself into a sheep and is pursued in the Wild Sheep Chase adventure. Ambitious researcher with poor judgment. *See: Wild Sheep Chase adventure*

**Noke's Tower**
Wizard's tower west of Waystone Inn. Site of Noke's polymorphing experiments. Contains spell components, research notes, magical traps, and aggressive sheep. *See: Wild Sheep Chase adventure*

**Northreach**
Frontier region of Aevoria. The player characters' starting location. Characterized by harsh winters, practical communities, and frontier independence. Home to the Waystone Inn (Northwatch Wardens headquarters). Known for resilience, hospitality to newcomers, and deep suspicion of authority. *See: Northreach chapter*

**Northwatch Wardens**
Adventuring guild based at Waystone Inn in Northreach. Operates on contract system—members take jobs and get paid. Leadership triad: Marshal Brenna Thorne (tactics), Steward Mara Fenwick (logistics), Lorewarden Elric Vael (research). Motto: "We watch. We act. We endure." *See: Northwatch Wardens chapter*

**Nullwood Expanse** (NULL-wood ex-PANS)
Elven-dominated forest region characterized by silence wards and memory preservation culture. Home to the Silent Court. Trees absorb sound; inhabitants speak in whispers and gestures. Values memory, records, and cautious change. *See: Regions chapter*

## R

**Red Covenant, The**
Disaster relief organization operating in the Emberlands and beyond. Provides aid, rebuilding assistance, and refugee support. Funded by donations and wealthy patrons. Viewed with deep respect—they work where others won't. *See: Organizations chapter*

**Resonance**
Dwarven cultural practice of listening to stone. Geomantic technique for understanding structural integrity, finding ore, and detecting danger. Both practical skill and spiritual connection to the Stonefather. *See: Stonebound Depths chapter*

**Rowan Fairweather**
Human bard and Northwatch Warden. Charismatic storyteller, morale booster, and negotiator. Performs at Waystone Inn, gathers rumors, handles diplomatic situations. "There's always a story here." Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

## S

**Salsvault** (SALZ-volt)
*Stress first syllable*
Buried Aeorian ruin far north of Palebank Village. Source of frigid woe spores and the Aeorian Echo phenomenon. Dangerous research site containing ancient magical experiments. *See: Frozen Sick adventure, The Far North chapter*

**Sera Gelanadel** (SAIR-uh gel-AN-uh-del)
*Elven name. Common mistake: "suh-RAH"*
High elf sorcerer and Northwatch Warden. Noble background, arcane expertise, diplomatic skills. Handles magical investigations and social situations. Polished but genuine. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Shepherd's Crook Inn**
Community gathering place in Welton. Local tavern and social hub run by welcoming proprietors. Source of village news and gossip. *See: Welton, Wolves of Welton adventure*

**Silent Court, The**
Governing body of the Nullwood Expanse. Elven council that preserves memory and protects the forest. Deliberate, cautious, values tradition. Communicates in elaborate sign language and whispers. Distrusts rapid change and outsiders. *See: Organizations chapter, Nullwood Expanse chapter*

**Solace Nexus** (SOH-luss NEX-us)
Ley-line port city in the Verdant Marches. Hub for regulated magical transportation and spellwork. Known for arcane bureaucracy, spell licensing, and magical trade. Efficient but paperwork-heavy. *See: Places of Legend chapter*

**Solaris** (soh-LAIR-iss)
*Region and city share the name*
Cultural capital of Aevoria and the Solaris Dominion. Center of arts, politics, bardic colleges, and courtly intrigue. Known for Eclipse Day commemoration, sophisticated culture, and complex social politics. *See: Regions chapter*

**Solaris Dominion**
Southern region of Aevoria centered on the city of Solaris. Characterized by cultural sophistication, strong Radiant Queen worship, and political complexity. Heartland of civilization and traditional power structures. *See: Regions chapter*

**Solace Trade Consortium**
Powerful merchant guild controlling most long-distance trade routes. Provides banking, contract enforcement, and trade negotiations. Essential for commerce but expensive. Viewed as reliable professionals or greedy monopolists depending on perspective. *See: Organizations chapter*

**Stonebound Assembly**
Governing council of the Stonebound Depths. Dwarven leadership based on clan representation and demonstrated craft mastery. Values tradition, quality work, and community stability. *See: Organizations chapter, Stonebound Depths chapter*

**Stonebound Depths**
Dwarven homeland beneath southern mountains. Underground realm of mines, forges, and great halls. Known for masterwork crafting, ancestral traditions, resonance geomancy, and stone expertise. Values endurance, quality, and honoring ancestors. *See: Regions chapter*

**Stonefather, The**
Dwarven deity of craft, endurance, and stone. Patron of artisans, miners, and those who build things to last. Emphasis on honest work and practical faith. *See: Gods and Religion chapter*

**Sunken Dominion**
Underwater realm of aquatic peoples (merfolk, tritons, sea elves). Explored through diving, water-breathing magic, or coastal settlements with mixed population. Known for beautiful coral architecture and mysterious deep-sea cultures. *See: Regions chapter*

## T

**Takk Oaksplitter** (TAK OAK-split-er)
*"Takk" rhymes with "back"*
Half-orc carpenter and Northwatch Warden. Skilled woodworker providing construction, repairs, and carpentry. Calm, methodical, takes pride in quality work. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Temple of the Dragonknights**
Cult stronghold in the northwest mountains of Northreach. Site of dragon-worshipping rituals and draconic power experiments. Location of capstone adventure for Season One. *See: Temple of the Dragonknights adventure*

**Tide Callers' Guild**
Maritime organization of sailors, navigators, and weather-watchers. Provide shipping services, coastal rescue, and navigation expertise. Essential for Shattered Coast and maritime trade. *See: Organizations chapter*

**Tillus Merrion** (TIL-us MAIR-ee-on)
Human council member in Welton. Practical village leader handling administrative matters and community decisions. Concerned about wolf attacks threatening his community. *See: People of Northreach chapter, Wolves of Welton adventure*

**Tulgi Lutan** (TOOL-gee LOO-tan)
*Goliath name. Stress first syllable of each name.*
Goliath healer in Palebank Village. Herbalist and practical medicine provider. Treats mundane injuries and illnesses with natural remedies. Caring but gruff. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

## U

**Unmasked Parliament**
Governing council of Vharoxis. Operates without masks (unusual for Vharoxis culture), signaling transparency in official capacity. Manages city infrastructure and mediates disputes. Limited power—much of Vharoxis operates outside official channels. *See: Organizations chapter, Vharoxis chapter*

**Urgon Wenth** (UR-gon WENTH)
*Dwarf name. Both parts rhyme with "fur-gone" and "tenth"*
Dwarf blacksmith in Palebank Village. Provides weapon repair, metal goods, and smithing services. Traditional craftsman maintaining dwarven quality standards. Reliable and fair. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

## V

**Vaeltharyn** (VALE-thar-in)
*Elven name. Stress first syllable.*
Capital city of the Nullwood Expanse. Great elven metropolis grown into living trees. Center of memory preservation and silent culture. Known for vast libraries, whisper chambers, and living architecture. *See: Nullwood Expanse chapter*

**Verdant Marches, The** (VER-dent MAR-chez)
Fertile farmland region of central Aevoria. Breadbasket of the continent. Known for agriculture, seasonal festivals, and nature-aligned worship. Home to Solace Nexus ley-line port. *See: Regions chapter*

**Verdant Mother, The**
Nature deity of growth, seasons, and balance. Patron of farmers, druids, and those living in harmony with natural cycles. Worship involves seasonal festivals and offerings. *See: Gods and Religion chapter*

**Vharoxis** (vah-ROX-iss)
*Stress middle syllable: "vah-ROX-iss"*
Outlaw city on the southern coast. Known for mask culture, black market trade, and absence of traditional law. "What you can keep is yours." Operates on reputation, contracts, and personal power. Dangerous but full of opportunities unavailable elsewhere. *See: Regions chapter*

## W

**Waystone Inn**
Headquarters of the Northwatch Wardens. Fortified inn serving as guild base, lodging, tavern, and community hub. Run by halfling chef Bordel Barleywind. Located centrally in Northreach territory. *See: Northwatch Wardens chapter*

**Welton**
Farming village southwest of Waystone Inn. Agricultural community experiencing mysterious wolf attacks in the Wolves of Welton adventure. Home to the Shepherd's Crook Inn. *See: Northreach chapter, Wolves of Welton adventure*

**Westly**
Shepherd in Welton whose farm is attacked by awakened wolves. Frightened but trying to protect his livelihood. Hires Northwatch Wardens to investigate. *See: Wolves of Welton adventure*

**Westly's Farm**
Sheep farm west of Welton. Site of initial wolf attacks that trigger the Wolves of Welton adventure. Remote location with vulnerable livestock. *See: Wolves of Welton adventure*

**Wild Sheep Chase, The**
Comic relief adventure involving polymorphing wizard Noke, his sheep-brother Finethir, and aggressive awakened sheep. Lighthearted tone but connects to campaign themes of destabilizing magic. *See: Wild Sheep Chase adventure*

**Willen Featherock**
Aarakocra scout and Northwatch Warden. Airborne reconnaissance specialist providing aerial perspective and scouting. Enthusiastic, helpful, sometimes fails to understand ground-based limitations. Based at Waystone Inn. *See: People of Northreach chapter*

**Wolves of Welton**
Adventure involving awakened wolves with developing intelligence attacking farms near Welton. First major indication of the Aeorian Echo phenomenon affecting wildlife. Moral complexity—wolves aren't evil, just hungry and confused. *See: Wolves of Welton adventure*
