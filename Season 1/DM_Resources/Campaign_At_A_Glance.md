# Campaign At A Glance

*One-page reference for the Northwatch Wardens campaign*

\page

## Campaign Premise

**Genre:** Frontier Guild Campaign  
**Setting:** Northreach, a wild frontier region of Aevoria  
**Structure:** Modular, order-independent adventures  
**Mystery:** The Aeorian Echo—ancient magic resurfacing  
**Levels:** 1-5 (expandable to 10+ in future seasons)

### What Makes It Special

- **Guild-Based:** Players are members of the Northwatch Wardens, a frontier protection guild
- **Drop-In Friendly:** Variable attendance supported; adventures work as one-shots or connected campaign
- **Player Choice:** Non-linear structure; players choose which contracts to take
- **Central Mystery:** All adventures connect to the Aeorian Echo investigation
- **Moral Complexity:** Few clear villains; many shades of gray

\page

## The Northwatch Wardens Guild

### Purpose
Protect Northreach's scattered settlements, investigate threats, and maintain order in a lawless frontier.

### Structure
- **Marshal Brenna Thorne:** Field commander, tactical decisions
- **Steward Mara Fenwick:** Quartermaster, logistics, daily operations  
- **Lorewarden Elric Vael:** Arcane scholar, historical research

### Headquarters
**Waystone Inn:** Central hub in Northreach. All adventures start and end here.

### How It Works
1. Guild leadership posts contracts
2. Wardens choose which to accept
3. Complete mission, return for payment
4. Repeat

\page

## Key NPCs (The Triad)

### Marshal Brenna Thorne
**Role:** Military commander, tactical leader  
**Personality:** Direct, practical, protective of wardens  
**Appearance:** Human woman, 40s, scarred veteran, practical armor  
**Voice:** Firm but not harsh, military bearing  
**Secret:** Haunted by command decisions that cost lives years ago  
**Motivation:** Protect the wardens and Northreach; never lose another squad

### Steward Mara Fenwick
**Role:** Quartermaster, guild logistics  
**Personality:** Warm, motherly, shrewd businesswoman  
**Appearance:** Halfling woman, 50s, aproned, always cooking or organizing  
**Voice:** Cheerful, gossipy, knows everyone's business  
**Secret:** Intelligence operative for The Broker (merchant network)  
**Motivation:** Keep the guild running smoothly; gather information for The Broker

### Lorewarden Elric Vael
**Role:** Arcane scholar, historian  
**Personality:** Curious, eccentric, socially awkward  
**Appearance:** Half-elf man, 60s, ink-stained fingers, too many books  
**Voice:** Rambling, excited about obscure facts, forgetful  
**Secret:** Obsessed with Aeorian artifacts; research may be dangerous  
**Motivation:** Understand the Aeorian Echo; unlock ancient secrets

## The Aeorian Echo (Core Mystery)

### What It Is
Ancient magical energy from buried Aeorian ruins (Salsvault) is spreading through Northreach, causing magical anomalies.

### Effects
- Intelligence awakening in animals (Wolves of Welton)
- Aeorian diseases spreading (Frozen Sick)
- Artifacts activating randomly
- Reality becoming unstable near Salsvault

### How Players Discover It
- **Gradually:** Each adventure provides 1-3 clues
- **In any order:** No single adventure is required first
- **Through investigation:** Reward player curiosity and research
- **From NPCs:** Elric is researching it; other NPCs notice strange events

### Resolution Path
Adventures → Investigation → Salsvault location discovered → Temple of the Dragonknights (faction exploiting the Echo) → Season 2 (dealing with Salsvault directly)

**See:** Mystery Revelation Guide for pacing details

## Adventures Overview

### Opening Adventures
**Open Skirmish** (Level 1, 1 hour)
- Wolf attack during "training exercise"
- Introduction to combat and guild
- **Echo Clue:** Wolves behaving strangely

**The Morning After** (Level 1, 30 min)
- Roleplay scene at Waystone Inn
- Meet the Triad and residents
- Receive first real contract

\page

### Main Adventures (In Any Order)

**Wolves of Welton** (Levels 1-3, 4-6 hours)
- **Type:** Investigation + moral choices
- **Location:** Welton village (southwest)
- **Threat:** Awakened wolves attacking livestock
- **Echo Clue:** Intelligence awakening in animals
- **Themes:** Nature vs civilization, sapience and rights
- **Recommended For:** First adventure; introduces investigation

**The Wild Sheep Chase** (Levels 1-2, 3-4 hours)
- **Type:** Comedy one-shot, chase scenes
- **Location:** Noke's Tower (west)
- **Threat:** Evil wizard stole Noke's body (Noke is now a sheep)
- **Echo Clue:** Polymorph magic destabilizing
- **Themes:** Lighthearted, high-magic chaos
- **Recommended For:** Palette cleanser between serious adventures

\page

**Peril in Pinebrook** (Levels 1-3, 4-6 hours)
- **Type:** Investigation + social
- **Location:** Pinebrook village (southeast)
- **Threat:** Disappearances, corrupt sheriff, bandits
- **Echo Clue:** Bandits found Aeorian artifacts
- **Themes:** Corruption, justice, frontier law
- **Recommended For:** Social-focused players

**Frozen Sick** (Levels 2-4, 6-8 hours)
- **Type:** Survival + exploration + body horror
- **Location:** Palebank Village and Croaker Cave (northeast)
- **Threat:** Aeorian disease spreading from ruins
- **Echo Clue:** Direct evidence of Salsvault and frigid woe disease
- **Themes:** Survival, sacrifice, contagion
- **Recommended For:** Groups that like darker themes

\page

**Temple of the Dragonknights** (Levels 4-5, 6-8 hours)
- **Type:** Combat + infiltration + capstone
- **Location:** Northwestern mountains
- **Threat:** Dragon cult exploiting the Aeorian Echo
- **Echo Clue:** Multiple factions aware of and exploiting the Echo
- **Themes:** Ambition, power, corruption
- **Recommended For:** Campaign finale or before advancing to Season 2

## Quick Reference Stats

### Party Scaling
**2 Players:** Reduce enemies by 30-40% or lower HP/damage
**3-4 Players:** Run as written
**5 Players:** Add 1-2 additional enemies or increase HP by 20-30%

**See:** Encounter Scaling Guide for detailed adjustments

### Milestone Leveling
- **Level 1 → 2:** After Open Skirmish
- **Level 2 → 3:** After 1-2 adventures
- **Level 3 → 4:** After 2-3 more adventures
- **Level 4 → 5:** Before Temple of the Dragonknights

**See:** Milestone Leveling for complete guidance

### Rewards (Per Adventure)
- **Gold:** 50-100 gp per player
- **Magic Items:** 1 common or uncommon per adventure
- **Reputation:** Increased standing with guild and factions
- **Downtime:** 5-10 days between contracts for training, crafting, carousing

**See:** Reward Guidelines for detailed recommendations

## Geography Quick Reference

### Locations (Distance from Waystone Inn)
- **Welton:** 1 day southwest (farming village)
- **Pinebrook:** 1.5 days southeast (trading village)
- **Palebank Village:** 2 days northeast (seaside settlement)
- **Noke's Tower:** 0.5 days west (wizard's tower)
- **Temple of the Dragonknights:** 2.5 days northwest (mountains)
- **Salsvault:** 3+ days far north (buried ruins, not visited in Season 1)

### Travel
- **Speed:** 24 miles/day on foot
- **Terrain:** Hills, forests, muddy spring roads
- **Encounters:** 1 in 6 chance per day (see Random Tables)
- **Weather:** Cold nights, mild days, sudden storms

## Campaign Tone

### What This Campaign Is
- Frontier survival with grounded stakes
- Investigation and discovery-focused
- Moral complexity and shades of gray
- Player-driven with meaningful choices
- Mystery gradually revealed

### What This Campaign Is Not
- High-magic power fantasy
- Clear good vs evil
- Linear railroad story
- Dungeon crawl focused
- Epic world-saving (yet—Season 1 is local)

### Running the Tone
- Emphasize isolation and frontier hardship
- NPCs have believable motivations, not just "evil"
- Magic is rare and mysterious, not commonplace
- Success through cleverness, not just combat power
- Consequences matter—both success and failure create stories

## Session Structure Template

### Opening (5-10 min)
1. Recap previous session
2. Set scene at Waystone Inn
3. NPC interactions or news
4. Hook for today's adventure

### Main Adventure (2-3 hours)
1. Travel to location (brief, summarize if boring)
2. Investigation/exploration
3. Social encounters with NPCs
4. Combat encounters (1-3 per session)
5. Discovery or revelation

### Wrap-Up (15-20 min)
1. Return to Waystone Inn
2. Debrief with Triad
3. Reward distribution
4. Downtime activities
5. Hook for next session (new contract posted)

**See:** Session Opening Templates for detailed examples

## DM Principles

### Core Rules
1. **Say "Yes, And..."** — Let players try creative solutions
2. **Fail Forward** — Failures create complications, not dead ends
3. **Show, Don't Tell** — Reveal through experience, not exposition
4. **NPCs Want Things** — Give every NPC a simple motivation
5. **Consequences Matter** — Both success and failure change the world

### When Improvising
- Use existing NPCs rather than inventing new ones
- Pull from World Secrets for lore consistency
- Reference Random Tables for quick ideas
- Let player speculation inspire you
- Trust your instincts

**See:** Improvisation Toolkit for detailed techniques

## Final Thoughts

This campaign is designed to be **flexible, forgiving, and fun**. 

You don't need to memorize everything. Reference this page during play, trust your instincts, and focus on your players having a good time.

**Remember:**
- Adventures work in any order
- Players drive the story through contract choices
- The mystery unfolds gradually—don't rush it
- Consequences create better stories than perfect plans
- If everyone's having fun, you're doing it right
