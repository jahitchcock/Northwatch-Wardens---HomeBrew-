# Troubleshooting Guide

*Solutions to common problems at the table*

\page

## Player Problems

### "Players ignore the contract"

**Solution:**
- Let them! Northreach has other threats
- Use random encounters
- Show consequences (village suffers without help)
- Other wardens take the contract
- Present more appealing hooks

**Don't:** Punish or railroad

\page

### "Players split the party"

**Solution:**
- Handle scenes sequentially
- Set timer for each scene
- Cut back and forth at tension points
- Let them coordinate via magic/NPCs

**If Dangerous:**
- Warn them of risk
- Make consequences clear
- Let dice fall where they may

\page

### "Player wants to kill important NPC"

**Solution:**
- Let them try
- NPCs have stats and can defend
- Other NPCs react (guild expels them, settlement hostile)
- Embrace chaos—create new problems to solve

**Don't:** Use plot armor

\page

### "Players are stuck/lost"

**Solutions:**
- Helpful NPC provides clue
- Elric can research and provide info
- "Actually, you remember..." (retcon minor detail)
- Skip to next interesting scene
- Fail forward—move plot despite missed clue

**Don't:** Let them spin wheels for an hour

\page

## Combat Problems

### "Combat too easy"

**Mid-Combat:**
- Add reinforcements
- Enemies use better tactics
- Boss reveals new ability

**Future Encounters:**
- More enemies
- Higher HP
- See Encounter Scaling Guide

\page

### "Combat too hard"

**Mid-Combat:**
- Enemies flee or surrender
- Friendly reinforcements arrive
- Environmental advantage appears
- Subtle HP reduction

**Future Encounters:**
- Fewer enemies
- Lower HP/damage
- See Encounter Scaling Guide

\page

### "Combat taking forever"

**Solutions:**
- Use average damage
- Set turn timer (1-2 minutes)
- Combine enemy turns
- Reduce enemy HP
- Enemies flee when half down

\page

## Pacing Problems

### "Session dragging"

**Solutions:**
- Skip travel montage
- Summarize shopping/prep
- Jump to next interesting scene
- Call for break
- End session early

\page

### "Players want to do everything"

**Solution:**
- Time pressure (contract is urgent)
- Diminishing returns (investigation exhausted)
- "You can do X or Y, not both"
- Focus on what matters

\page

## Story Problems

### "Players derail the plot"

**Solution:**
- There is no plot, only consequences
- Let them derail
- Adjust future content
- Improvise new direction
- Use Improvisation Toolkit

**Remember:** Player agency > your plan

\page

### "Players solved it 'wrong way'"

**Solution:**
- Their solution IS the right way
- Award success
- Adjust consequences
- Learn for next time

**Example:** Players negotiate instead of fight—great! Adjust rewards and consequences.

\page

### "I didn't prep this content"

**Solutions:**
- Use Random Tables
- Recycle unused content from other adventures
- Ask players to describe (shift spotlight)
- Take 5-minute break to think
- "Let's pick this up next session" (if truly stuck)

\page

## Mystery Problems

### "Players figured it out too early"

**Solution:**
- Reward them! They're smart.
- Confirm their theory
- New question: What do they do about it?
- Still have adventures to complete

**Don't:** Invalidate their deduction

\page

### "Players completely miss the mystery"

**Solution:**
- That's okay! Focus on adventures.
- Elric can provide exposition later
- Mystery emerges naturally over time
- Temple brings it to forefront

**Don't:** Force it

\page

## Rules Problems

### "I don't know the rule"

**Solution:**
- Make ruling, look it up later
- Ask players if they know
- Use common sense
- Consistency > correctness

\page

### "Player argues ruling"

**Solution:**
- "I hear you. My ruling stands for now. We'll discuss after session."
- Stay calm
- Don't argue at table
- Discuss privately later

\page

## Table Dynamics

### "One player dominates"

**Solutions:**
- "Player X, what do you think?"
- Spotlight mechanics (turns, rounds)
- Side scenes for quiet players
- Talk to player privately

\page

### "Player on phone/distracted"

**Solutions:**
- "Player X, you're up" (bring them back)
- Talk privately if persistent
- Set table expectations Session 0

\page

### "Players aren't having fun"

**Solutions:**
- Check in directly: "How's everyone feeling?"
- Adjust content (more combat? more RP?)
- Take break
- End early if needed
- Debrief after session

\page

## DM Problems

### "I'm not prepared"

**Solution:**
- Be honest: "I'm underprepared, let's do a short session"
- Use Random Tables
- Run Wild Sheep Chase (easy to run cold)
- Roleplay-heavy session at Waystone Inn
- Reschedule if needed

\page

### "I made a mistake"

**Solution:**
- Acknowledge it
- Retcon if necessary
- "I misspoke earlier, actually..."
- Learn for next time
- It's okay—everyone makes mistakes

\page

### "I'm not having fun"

**Solution:**
- Take a break
- Discuss with players
- Adjust style
- It's okay to end a campaign
- Your fun matters too

\page

## When in Doubt

**Remember:**
1. Player fun > rules
2. Story > mechanics
3. Consequences > railroading
4. Improvise > stall
5. Ask players > struggle alone

**You've got this. Trust yourself.**
