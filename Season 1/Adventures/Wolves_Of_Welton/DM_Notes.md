# Wolves of Welton - DM Notes

*Quick reference and guidance for running this adventure*

\page

## Quick Reference

**Recommended Level:** 1-3 (ideal for level 2)  
**Session Length:** 4-6 hours (or 2 shorter sessions)  
**Party Size:** 3-4 players (scalable to 2-5)  
**Key NPCs:** Mathilde (village elder), Father Merrikson (priest), Alexi (awakened wolf pack leader)  
**Echo Clue:** Intelligence awakening in animals due to transmutation magic  
**Primary Location:** Welton village (southwest of Waystone Inn)

\page

## The Big Picture

### What's Really Happening

**The Truth:**
- Wolves have gained sapient intelligence due to the Aeorian Echo
- Led by an awakened wolf named "Alexi" (actually a transformed sorcerer)
- Wolves are stealing food to survive, not attacking out of malice
- They're struggling with newfound intelligence and morality
- Some wolves want peace, others want to dominate

**The Twist:**
- There's no clear "villain"
- Players must navigate a moral dilemma
- Multiple resolution paths (combat, negotiation, compromise)
- Choices have lasting consequences for Welton and the wolves

\page

### Echo Connection

**What Players Learn:**
- Wolves showing unusual intelligence and behavior
- Coordinated tactics beyond normal animal cunning
- Some can understand speech (and one can speak through magic)
- Transformation happened gradually over 3 months
- Concentrated in this region (near Salsvault, though players don't know that yet)

**What Elric Will Want:**
- Wolf samples for study
- Details about their behavior
- Any magical artifacts or anomalies found
- Information about when transformation began

\page

## Three Major Scenes

### Scene 1: Road Ambush

**What Happens:** Wolf pack attacks shepherds driving sheep

**Purpose:**
- First encounter with intelligent wolves
- Demonstrate unusual tactics
- Meet grateful villagers who guide party to Welton

**Combat Notes:**
- 8 wolves (scale for party size)
- Wolves are stealing sheep, not killing people
- Will retreat if outmatched (intelligent, not suicidal)
- Shepherds grateful, share rumors

**Scaling:**
- 2 players: 5 wolves
- 3 players: 6-7 wolves
- 4 players: 8 wolves (as written)
- 5 players: 9-10 wolves

\page

### Scene 2: Welton Village

**What Happens:** Investigation and information gathering

**Purpose:**
- Learn about wolf attacks
- Meet village council
- Interview witnesses (including Willen Featherock)
- Discover sorcerer Alexi went missing

**Key NPCs:**
- **Mathilde:** Village elder, pragmatic, worried about food security
- **Father Merrikson:** Priest, his brother Alexi is missing
- **Tillus Merrion:** Halfling merchant, wants immediate action
- **Willen Featherock:** Shepherd traumatized by wolves, "hears voices"
- **Leanor Slatebeard:** Dwarf innkeeper, offers free food and lodging

**Investigation Clues:**
- Wolves open gates and doors (intelligent)
- Use ambush tactics (coordinated)
- Steal food but avoid killing (not typical predator behavior)
- Attacks started 3 months ago (timing important for Echo)
- Willen mentions "voices in the night" (wolves talking to each other)

\page

### Scene 3: Forest Confrontation

**What Happens:** Party finds wolf den and confronts pack

**Purpose:**
- Reveal wolves are sapient
- Present moral dilemma
- Multiple resolution paths
- Determine Welton's future

**The Wolf Pack:**
- Led by "Alexi" (large alpha wolf who was actually the sorcerer, polymorphed permanently)
- Mixed opinions: some wolves want peace, others want to drive humans away
- Desperately need food but don't want to become "monsters"
- Fear humans will hunt them to extinction

**Alexi (Alpha Wolf):**
- Can cast some spells (limited sorcerer abilities)
- Intelligent but still partially wolf in thinking
- Wants to protect pack but willing to negotiate
- Brother is Father Merrikson (emotional complication)

\page

## Resolution Paths

### Path 1: Negotiated Peace

**How:** Players talk to wolves, arrange compromise

**Compromise Options:**
- Welton provides food in exchange for wolves protecting against bandits/monsters
- Wolves hunt in different territory, away from Welton
- Split territory: wolves get northern forest, humans get farmland
- Temporary truce while seeking cure for Alexi

**Outcomes:**
- Wolves and village coexist peacefully
- Welton gains unusual allies
- Father Merrikson reunited with brother (though Alexi is still a wolf)
- Echo mystery deepens (what caused this?)

**Rewards:**
- 100 gp from village
- Reputation increase with Welton
- Unique reward: wolves as allies (can call for help in future)
- Elric very interested in studying phenomenon

\page

### Path 2: All-Out Combat

**How:** Players fight and kill the wolf pack

**Combat:**
- 12-15 wolves + Alpha (Alexi)
- Brutal, difficult fight
- Wolves fight to the death protecting their home

**Outcomes:**
- Wolves defeated, threat eliminated
- Village safe but at moral cost
- Father Merrikson devastated (brother killed)
- Echo mystery remains (what caused awakening?)

**Rewards:**
- 100 gp from village
- Reputation increase with Welton
- Wolf pelts and meat
- Hollow victory (killed sapient beings)

\page

### Path 3: Selective Elimination

**How:** Players kill aggressive wolves, spare peaceful ones

**Process:**
- Identify which wolves want war vs. peace
- Eliminate war faction (5-7 wolves)
- Negotiate with remaining wolves

**Outcomes:**
- Compromise achieved through violence
- Village safe, some wolves survive
- Father Merrikson conflicted (violence used but brother saved)
- Balance between paths 1 and 2

**Rewards:**
- 100 gp from village
- Reputation with Welton
- Some wolf allies remain
- Moral complexity (was violence necessary?)

\page

### Path 4: Finding a Cure

**How:** Players seek to reverse transformation

**Process:**
- Research with Elric (takes time, between sessions)
- Find rare components
- Cast ritual to restore wolves to normal OR Alexi to human form

**Outcomes:**
- Wolves return to normal animals, no longer intelligent
- Alexi restored to human form (if focused on him)
- Village safe, no moral compromise
- Significant time and resource investment

**Rewards:**
- 100 gp + bonus from grateful Father Merrikson (50 gp)
- Reputation increase
- Elric's gratitude (future research help)
- "Good ending" but requires work

## Running the Investigation

### Information Sources

**Villagers (Common Knowledge):**
- Wolves unusually effective
- Started 3 months ago
- Alexi disappeared at same time
- Previous hunting party failed, one survivor (Willen)

**Village Council (Official Version):**
- Economic impact severe (can't export food, people starving)
- 100 gp reward for solving problem
- Want wolves dead or driven away
- Desperate for solution

**Willen Featherock (Traumatized Witness):**
- Attacked by wolves in forest
- Heard "voices in the night" (wolves talking)
- Saw "thunder and fire" (Alexi's spells)
- Wolves dragged him to edge of forest, didn't kill him (mercy)
- Other hunters think he's crazy

**Father Merrikson (Personal Connection):**
- Brother Alexi vanished same time attacks started
- Alexi was sorcerer (transmutation specialist)
- Merrikson suspects connection but doesn't know what
- Will plead for brother's life if Alexi revealed

\page

### Investigation Skill Checks

**Nature/Survival (DC 12):**
- Wolf tracks show unusual patterns (coordinated, planned)
- Marks on gates suggest intelligent manipulation, not animal damage

**Investigation (DC 15):**
- Timeline: Alexi disappeared exactly when attacks started
- Wolves deliberately avoid killing people
- Food stolen matches what pack would need to survive

**Arcana (DC 13):**
- Magical residue in areas of attacks (transmutation school)
- Similar to polymorph magic but permanent

**Insight (talking to Willen, DC 12):**
- He's telling the truth about voices
- He's not crazy, he's traumatized
- Wolves deliberately spared him (unusual for animals)

## Scaling for Party Size

### Combat Encounters

**Road Ambush (Scene 1):**
- 2 players: 5 wolves
- 3 players: 6-7 wolves
- 4 players: 8 wolves (as written)
- 5 players: 9-10 wolves

**Forest Confrontation (Scene 3, if combat):**
- 2 players: 8 wolves + Alpha (reduce Alpha HP by 40%)
- 3 players: 10 wolves + Alpha (reduce Alpha HP by 20%)
- 4 players: 12 wolves + Alpha (as written)
- 5 players: 15 wolves + Alpha (increase Alpha HP by 30%)

**Alpha Wolf (Alexi) Stats:**
- Use Dire Wolf stat block + 2-3 sorcerer spells
- HP: 37 (reduce for small parties, increase for large)
- Spells: Thunderwave, Burning Hands, Shield
- Intelligent: will negotiate, flee if losing, fight to protect pack

## Common Player Approaches

### "We want to talk to the wolves"

**Perfect!** This is the intended moral complexity.

**How to Run:**
- Wolves are initially suspicious/hostile
- Alexi steps forward, casts Message or Comprehend Languages
- Conversation happens (Alexi can "speak" through magic)
- Players learn truth, must decide what to do

**Let players drive negotiation. Wolves are desperate, not evil.**

\page

### "We want to kill all the wolves"

**Also valid.** This is a legitimate choice.

**How to Run:**
- Difficult combat (12-15 wolves + Alpha)
- Wolves fight desperately
- Last few wolves (including Alexi) try to negotiate or flee
- Father Merrikson devastated if Alexi dies
- Village relieved but players may feel moral weight

**Let players face consequences. This isn't "wrong" but it's complicated.**

\page

### "We want to cure the wolves"

**Creative solution!** Requires work.

**How to Run:**
- Elric needs time to research (1-2 days of downtime)
- Requires rare components (quest in itself)
- Ritual casting scene
- Choice: reverse all wolves or just Alexi?
- Feel-good ending but significant investment

**Reward creativity. This is harder but more satisfying.**

\page

### "We want to blame someone else"

**Players might suspect:**
- Father Merrikson (no, he's just worried about his brother)
- Village council (no, they're just desperate)
- Enemy nation/cult (no, this is Echo effects)
- Someone deliberately cursed the wolves (no, it's ambient magic)

**How to Handle:**
- Investigation reveals no conspiracy
- Echo is environmental, not intentional
- Sometimes there's no villain, just a situation

## Roleplaying Key NPCs

### Alexi (Alpha Wolf)

**Voice:** Strained, struggling to form words (using magic to communicate)

**Personality:**
- Intelligent but thinking partially like a wolf now
- Protective of pack (his "family" now)
- Guilty about stealing but desperate to feed pack
- Misses humanity but can't return
- Will negotiate but won't abandon pack

**Key Lines:**
- "We... don't want... to hurt anyone. We're hungry. Need... to survive."
- "I was... human. Merrikson's brother. Something... changed me. Changed us all."
- "Please. We'll leave. Just... don't kill my pack."

\page

### Father Merrikson

**Voice:** Gentle, worried, educated

**Personality:**
- Compassionate priest who wants everyone safe
- Desperate to find brother
- Torn between duty to village and love for Alexi
- Will plead for mercy if Alexi revealed
- Grateful no matter the outcome

**Key Lines:**
- "My brother vanished three months ago. I fear something terrible has happened."
- "The village needs protection, but... surely there's a way without more bloodshed?"
- "If you find Alexi, please... tell him I don't blame him."

\page

### Mathilde (Village Elder)

**Voice:** Practical, no-nonsense, local accent

**Personality:**
- Pragmatic leader focused on village survival
- Not cruel but prioritizes her people
- Will accept any solution that works
- Appreciates competence

**Key Lines:**
- "We need results. People are starving."

## Complications & Twists

### If Players Kill Alexi Before Learning Truth

- Father Merrikson identifies brother's body
- Deep regret for players (didn't know)
- Investigation reveals truth post-mortem
- Lesson about gathering information first
- Village still grateful but moment is bittersweet

\page

### If Players Reveal Truth to Father Merrikson

- He rushes to forest to see brother
- Emotional reunion (even though Alexi is a wolf)
- Merrikson pleads with council to spare Alexi
- Village torn: safety vs. mercy
- Players' recommendation carries weight

\page

### If Wolves Reject Negotiation

- Aggressive wolves overrule Alexi
- Combat becomes necessary (not player fault)
- Alexi might fight alongside players against own pack
- Tragic outcome: intelligence didn't make them better, just more dangerous

\page

### If Players Try to Bring Wolves Back to Waystone Inn

- Brenna skeptical but interested
- Elric fascinated, wants to study them
- Mara worried about other guests
- Can work if wolves are peaceful
- Becomes long-term ally option

## Session Breakdown

### Session 1 (2-3 hours)

1. **Travel to Welton** (summarize, 5 min)
2. **Road Ambush** (Scene 1, 45 min)
3. **Arrive in Welton** (Scene 2 start, 15 min)
4. **Village Investigation** (Scene 2 continuation, 60-90 min)
   - Interview council
   - Talk to Willen Featherock
   - Gather rumors
5. **End session:** Prepare to enter forest

\page

### Session 2 (2-3 hours)

1. **Recap** (5 min)
2. **Travel to forest** (random encounter optional, 20 min)
3. **Find wolf den** (Scene 3 start, 15 min)
4. **Confrontation** (Scene 3 conclusion, 60-90 min)
   - Negotiation or combat
   - Resolution
5. **Return to Welton** (20 min)
6. **Debrief and rewards** (15 min)

## Echo Revelation Timing

**During Adventure:**
- Wolves show unusual intelligence (immediately obvious)
- Players can investigate and discover magical cause
- Elric interested when they report back

**After Adventure:**
- Elric analyzes samples (if provided)
- Confirms transmutation magic
- Mentions "arcane resonance" in region
- First real clue about Echo (if this is first adventure)

**Don't over-explain.** Let mystery simmer. This is one piece of a larger puzzle.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Force a specific resolution (let players choose)
- Make wolves obviously evil (they're desperate, not malicious)
- Reveal truth too early (let investigation happen)
- Kill Alexi before reveal (rob emotional impact)
- Ignore Father Merrikson's role (he's emotionally central)

**Do:**
- Present multiple valid solutions
- Make consequences clear
- Show wolves' humanity (intelligence, morality, fear)
- Reward creative problem-solving
- Let players struggle with moral complexity

\page

## Troubleshooting

### "Players immediately want to kill wolves"

Let them try. Make combat difficult. When wolves start dying, have survivors plead for mercy. Reveal intelligence through desperation.

### "Players want to take too long investigating"

Time pressure: wolves attack another farm, villagers starving, council pressures for action.

### "Players kill Alexi before learning he's the sorcerer"

Father Merrikson identifies body. Players learn truth post-mortem. Consequences: regret, lesson learned. Still get paid.

### "Players try to bring wolves into village"

Village panics. Council forbids it. Negotiation must happen at forest border, not in town. Wolves won't enter Welton anyway (fear).

### "Players want to side with wolves against villagers"

Valid but extreme. Villages needs food. Wolves need food. Forcing village to give everything causes starvation. Negotiate balanced solution.

## Final Thoughts

**Wolves of Welton is about moral complexity, not combat.**

The "best" outcome isn't predefined. Let players struggle with the choice:
- Kill intelligent beings to save village?
- Risk village to spare wolves?
- Find compromise that works for both?

**Every choice has consequences. Every consequence tells a story.**

This adventure teaches players:
- Northreach is complicated
- Not every threat is evil
- Investigation matters
- Choices have weight
- The Echo changes things in unexpected ways

**Trust your players. Let them surprise you.**
